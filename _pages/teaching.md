## Welding with the UCSD Steel Bridge Team

Although this wasn't a formal role, I was heavily involved with training new members to weld when I was co-project manager of the UCSD steel bridge team . I personally trained approx 60 undergrads to weld in (many) small groups of 2-4 and supervised the training process for an additional 40. This involved shop safety, technical knowledge of welding, and experience with small-group instruction. Civil engineering students don't always get the chance to be hands-on with the techniques that are actually used to make structural connections so these lessons were particularly fun for everyone involved.

## Structural testing with the UCSD Steel Bridge Team

As another informal role, I developed and led the load testing program of the UCSD steel bridge team and trained students how to run structural tests. This involved coordination with UCSD's [Powell Structural Laboratory](https://se.ucsd.edu/facilities/structural-systems-laboratory) to schedule tests (shoutout to Mike!), technical skills to get the DAQ and sensors working, and organizational skills to get everything where it needed to be on the day of the test. I instructed students on how to design and plan tests, how to select and install sensors, and how to interpret results. Over the two years I was in this role, I worked with about 55 students.

Students that participated in load testing got physical intuition about structural deflection and loading, experience with sensors and data aquisition, and an appreciation for how simulated structural behavior doesn't always match real life.
